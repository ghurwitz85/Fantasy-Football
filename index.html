Sources & trust weighting

How blending works

Each source's rankings are converted to a percentile (where a player sits within that source's own list), then combined as a weighted average using the trust weight on each source card. A player only found in one source is scored on that source alone. Where sources disagree a lot on a player, that disagreement itself feeds into the Risk Tolerance adjustment below, alongside FantasyPros' own expert-disagreement figure.

FantasyPros supports live fetch (via the Worker) since its rankings sit in a fairly parseable page. Draft Sharks and any source you add are CSV-paste only — Draft Sharks' full list sits behind their paywall for logged-in subscribers, so export it yourself from your account and paste it in here rather than something scraping it on your behalf.

How the math works

Scarcity compares your league's per-team starter demand at each position (including a flex share) against a standard 12-team/1QB/2RB/2WR/1TE/1FLEX baseline, and shifts ranks toward positions your league makes scarcer.

Risk tolerance combines FantasyPros' expert-disagreement (rank std. dev.) with cross-source disagreement as a boom/bust proxy — high disagreement means a wide range of likely outcomes.

Injury and rookie weighting only apply to players you've flagged in the table below — flag them, then use the sliders to control how much that flag matters.

O-line, QB-support, strength-of-schedule, and game-script weights now flow through the V3 team-context model. Big-play confidence controls how much the V3 model trusts projected 40+ yard event counts before applying your league's actual bonus scoring; it does not multiply the scoring rule itself. Cached normalized QB, defense, and game-script scores are active for all 32 teams; the table below remains as a transparent manual fallback for legacy rank-style O-line and schedule inputs.

How Projected Points and VORP work

Projected Points = your pasted raw stats run through the League Scoring Rules above — accurate to your exact league, not a generic PPR number. If both a current-season projection and a historical stat line are loaded for a player, they're blended by the History Weight slider; with only one loaded, that one is used at full weight.

VORP (Value Over Replacement Player) compares a player's projected points to the projected points of the last realistic starter at his position in your league — using the same per-team demand math as the scarcity slider. This is the standard way to compare value across positions for draft decisions: it's what actually maximizes your starting lineup's total expected points, which raw points or rank alone can't tell you (a mediocre RB can be worth more than a good TE if the RB replacement level is much worse).

Important: players with no projection or history loaded get zero contribution from this slider — the rest of your weighting still applies. If the VORP column is blank/zero, load projection CSVs or historical CSVs first.
